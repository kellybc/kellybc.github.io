Microphone Basics

Your Microphone

The microphone in your kit is known as an electret microphone. An electret is a dielectric material that possesses a permanent or semipermanent electric polarity, analogous to a permanent magnet. The key component of the microphone is this diaphragm that when acoustic waves strike it, it produces a voltage output. This voltage caused by the deflection of the diaphragm may then be measured by the Arduino. The microphone in your kit was purchased from SparkFun found here. From the datasheet, it may seen that the microphone has an operating voltage range from $ 1 VDC $ to $ 10 VDC $, a maximum current draw of $ 0.5 mA $, and responses frequencies in the range of $ 100 $ to $ 10,000 Hz $. The microphone anatomy is shown below in Figure 1 comes from the datasheet as well.

Figure 1: Microphone Diagram

The various elements of Figure 1 are listed below.

  1. This portion is a dustproof gauze layer that protects the internal components of the microphone.
  2. The case is composed of an aluminum alloy.
  3. The diaphragm is the internal component of the microphone that interacts with the acoustic waves.
  4. This portion is a spacer.
  5. This portion is the electret plate that produces the voltage oscillations whenever it is deflected.
  6. This portion is called the housing chamber and keeps all the internal components in place.
  7. This layer is a printed circuit board (PCB) used to make all the electrical connections.
  8. This portion is known as the FET (field effect transistor). It is special type of transistor which uses an electric field to control the flow of current.
  9. These are the connection pins used to connect the microphone to a circuit.

It should be noted that electret microphones do have a polarity. Figure 2 shows a diagram (taken from the datasheet) of the microphone from your kit.

Figure 2: Microphone Diagram

It may be seen that terminal 2 is the ground pin. The easiest way to identify this pin on the actual device is to notice the pad surrounding each pin on the microphone. The pin that has the pad that connects to the microphone's outer casing is the ground pin. This connection is shown in the diagram in Figure 2. The other terminal has a pad that is isolated from everything else. It looks like an island. Whenever implementing the microphone in a circuit, be sure to get the correct orientation of the pins; otherwise, it will not work correctly.
