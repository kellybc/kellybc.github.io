17.Student Distribution
¶

If the normal distribution is to populations, then the Student distribution is to samples

sample mean may differ from the population mean

different samples from the same population could produce different means

The same applies to the standard deviation

the sample mean and standard deviation are not a good estimate of the population mean and standard deviation

The Student distribution may be thought of as the best guess of the population's normal distribution

The more data that is collected, the better the parent population is understood

the Student distribution approaches that of the normal distribution as the number of data points in the sample increases

The Student distribution depends on two parameters

$$ \nu = n - 1 $$

where $ n $ is the number of data points

The probability density function (PDF) for a Student distribution is

$$ P\left(t, \nu \right) = \frac{\Gamma \left( \frac{\nu + 1}{2} \right)}{\sqrt{\nu \pi}\left(\frac{\nu}{2}\right)\left(1+\frac{t^2}{\nu}\right)^\frac{\nu+1}{2}} $$
$$ \Gamma \left( x \right) = \left(x - 1\right)! $$
