16.Normal Distribution
¶

most common distribution

A bell curve is another common name

describes the behavior of a continuous random variable

an equal probability that there will be positive and negative deviations

described by three parameters

$$ P\left( x, \mu, \sigma \right) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{\frac{-\left(x-\mu \right)^2}{2\sigma^2}} $$

known as the probability density function or PDF

Figure 1: Normal distributions with different $ \sigma $'s
Figure 2: Normal distributions with different $ \mu $'s

the probability that $ x $ will fall between $ a $ and $ b $ is given by

$$ P \left(a \le x \le b \right) = \int_a^b P\left(x\right) dx$$

where $ P\left(x\right) $ is the PDF

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} P \left( x \right) dx = 1 $$

The transformation is

$$ z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} $$

where $ z $ is now the continuous random variable

The transformation reduces the PDF to

$$ P\left( z \right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^\frac{-z^2}{2} $$

  2. For a mean of $ 100 $, and a standard deviation of $ 37 $, what are the probabilities that the values of $ 50 $, $ 75 $, and $ 140 $ or anything less than those values will occur?
  3. For a mean of $ 42 $, and a standard deviation of $ 7 $, what is the range of random variables that correspond to the probabilities of $ 0.66 $, $ 0.75 $, and $ 0.95 $?
